The Terrestrial  Planets

  1. Mercury
    • Almost Tidally Locked 3:2 spin.
    • Days are longer then years.
    • 0.38G or 3.7 m/s2.
    • Surface Temperature ranges from -279°F to 801°F.
    • Atmospheric Pressure is 10-12 bar.
  2. Venus
    • Runaway Greenhouse Effect.
    • Venus spins backwards, or clockwise.
    • 0.91G or 8.87 m/s2.
    • Surface Temperature ranges from 820°F to 900°F.
    • Atmospheric Pressure is 95 bars.
  3. Earth
    • Days are slowly getting longer.
    • Gravitational Fields change based on location.
    • 1.00G or 9.81 m/s2.
    • Surface Temperature ranges from -129°F to 136°F.
    • Atmospheric Pressure is 1.01 bar.
  4. Mars
    • Very Thin Atmosphere.
    • Sunsets on Mars are blue.
    • 0.38 G's or 3.72 m/s2.
    • Surface Temperature ranges from -125°F to 23°F.
    • Atmospheric Pressure is 0.006 bar.

Mars Information

Mars is the fourth planet from the sun. Befitting the Red Planet's bloody color, the Romans named it after their god of war. In truth, the Romans copied the ancient Greeks, who also named the planet after their god of war, Ares.

Other civilizations also typically gave the planet names based on its color — for example, the Egyptians named it "Her Des-her," meaning "the red one," while ancient Chinese astronomers dubbed it "the fire star."
